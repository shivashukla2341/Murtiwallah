How to Choose a Brass Murti

By the Murtiwallah Craft Desk · 12 Jul 2026 · 6 min read

Brass has been the material of choice for Indian murtis for centuries — it takes fine detail well, ages into a warm patina, and survives generations of handling. But not all brass murtis are made equally, and knowing what to check protects you from a piece that looks right in a photo and disappoints in hand.

1. Check the Casting Method

Lost-wax casting produces sharper detail in the face, jewellery and drapery than sand-casting. Run a finger along the ornamentation — lost-wax pieces feel crisp at the edges, while sand-cast pieces often feel slightly rounded or soft in the finer lines.

2. Weight Is a Purity Signal

A genuine solid-brass murti has real heft. Pieces that feel unusually light for their size may be hollow-cast with thin walls, or blended with a higher zinc content that dulls the gold tone over time. Ask your dealer for the weight-per-inch benchmark for the specific deity and pose.

3. Finish and Patina

Antique-gold and dark-patina finishes should sit evenly in every recess — uneven patina pooling in creases is a sign of a rushed finishing pass. A well-finished murti still shows a hint of bright brass at the raised highlights, where hand-polishing would naturally catch the light.

4. Ask for a MOQ Sample

For wholesale and temple orders, always request one finished sample before confirming a bulk run. Reputable workshops — Murtiwallah included — will ship or photograph a sample piece so you can verify casting quality before a container-load commitment.
